Once you've decided who you want to build your new custom frame, the next step is to contact them. It's best to use email or the contact form on their website. Each builder will have their own order process and it can take up to 12 months from start to the end.
Here's the key parts to work out:

Type of frame:

Fully custom - completely to your specs. Tube lengths, materials etc.
Semi custom - made to order in set sizes and materials.

Material:

Most custom frames are steel but titanium is also popular. There are a few people that build with carbon fiber and aluminium too. Your frame builder will be able to advise the best type of each material to achieve the handling and feel that you want.

Terrain:

There is not just road, cyclocross, mountain bike or bmx frames, but also the type of surface you expect to be riding on will affect the specifications.

Finishing kit:

You can get the frame made to fit exactly the finishing kit you want. Tyre clearance, internal cabling, cable, electronic or wireless gears, dynamo hubs and lights etc. can all be integrated seamlessly.

Bike fit:

A bike fit is the only way to work out the exact measurements you need. They are often included with the price of the frame, some builders do their own and others have partnerships with a local shop. It is also possible to get a bike fit separately or use the details of a previous one.
